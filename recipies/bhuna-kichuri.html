Bhuna Khichuri

Description

A dry, roasted version of the traditional rice and lentil porridge, served with deep-fried eggs.

Ingredients

  • Roasted Moong Dal
  • Kalojira rice
  • Ghee
  • Whole spices
  • Eggs

steps

  1. Roast the Dal: Dry roast the Moong Dal in a pan on medium heat until it turns golden brown and smells nutty. Wash it and set aside.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: Heat ghee or oil in a pot. Add whole spices (bay leaf, cloves, cardamom, cinnamon) and sliced onions. Fry until the onions are translucent.
  3. Fry Rice and Dal: Add the washed Kalojira rice and roasted dal to the pot. Stir-fry them together for 3–4 minutes; this ensures the Khichuri stays grainy and "Bhuna."
  4. Season: Stir in turmeric, ginger paste, and salt.
  5. Boil: Add hot water and bring to a boil. Once the water starts to dry up, tuck in a few whole green chilies for flavor.
  6. Final Steam: Cover the pot tightly with a lid. Cook on very low heat for 15 minutes.
  7. Fry the Eggs: While the rice steams, boil eggs, peel them, score the sides, and deep-fry them with a pinch of salt and turmeric until the skin is crispy and golden. Serve them on top of the Khichuri.
